We've all been there - a new client signs, and suddenly someone on your team spends the next two days chasing paperwork.

I worked with an agency recently where onboarding a single client took four separate emails, a welcome PDF someone edited by hand every time, a contract sent through a different tool, and a kickoff call booked by back-and-forth messages. Multiply that by 6 new clients a month, and one person was losing almost a full week just getting people started.

Here's the part that stung: none of it required judgment. It was the same five steps, in the same order, every time. Send the welcome email. Send the contract. Collect the intake form. Book the kickoff call. Add them to the project tool. A checklist, basically - just done manually, every single time.

Here's what we did together: we built a simple automation that kicks off the moment a deal closes. The welcome email goes out immediately, personalized with the client's name and package. The contract sends itself through e-signature. Once it's signed, the intake form goes out automatically. And the second that form is submitted, a kickoff call link appears in the client's inbox and the project gets created in their project management tool - no one on the team has to touch it.

Total setup time: about a day and a half, mostly spent connecting the tools they already had. Time saved per client: roughly 3 hours. Across 6 clients a month, that's 18 hours back - more than two full workdays.

But the real win was what it did to the client's first impression. New clients used to wait a day or two between signing and hearing anything real. Now they get a polished, fast, professional welcome within minutes. Clients started commenting on it unprompted - "wow, that was fast."

That's the thing about onboarding automation. It's not just an internal time-saver. It's often the client's very first experience with how you work.
